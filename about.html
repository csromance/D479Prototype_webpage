Welcome to Taniti

Taniti is a small tropical island in the Pacific with lush rainforests, black- and white-sand beaches, and a close-knit community. Visitors can spend their days hiking, snorkeling, or exploring markets, then unwind in the evening with local music and seaside dining.

Quick Facts

Island Overview

Taniti’s main town sits along a protected lagoon, with small fishing villages and rainforest trails radiating out from the harbor. Most lodging, dining, and guided tours are within a short walk or shuttle ride of the waterfront.

History & Culture

The island’s culture blends long-standing local traditions with influences from seafaring traders and modern visitors. Guests are encouraged to join village festivals, sample family-run food stalls, and follow local customs of greeting and dress.

Tourism Mission

Taniti’s tourism board focuses on small-scale, sustainable travel that supports local businesses and protects the island’s reefs, forests, and wildlife. Visitor spending helps fund conservation projects and community programs across the island.
