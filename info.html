About the Island

Discover the natural beauty and rich culture of Taniti.

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Dining

From local cuisine to international flavors.

Restaurants

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants offering diverse dining options:

  • Five restaurants serve mostly local fish and rice
  • Three restaurants serve American-style meals
  • Two restaurants serve Pan-Asian cuisine

Grocery Stores

For those who prefer to cook or need supplies:

  • Two supermarkets for full grocery shopping
  • Two smaller grocery stores for quick essentials
  • One convenience store open 24 hours a day

Lodging

Accommodation options for every budget and preference.

Taniti offers a wide variety of lodging options to suit every traveler:

  • An inexpensive hostel for budget travelers
  • One large, four-star resort for luxury seekers
  • Many small, family-owned hotels
  • A growing number of bed and breakfasts

All types of lodging are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government to ensure quality and safety standards.

Sightseeing

Popular tourist destinations and activities.

Taniti City

Most tourists spend most of their time in Taniti City, which boasts native architecture and nearby white, sandy beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay. The city offers an authentic cultural experience while providing modern amenities.

Popular Activities

  • Boat tours of the island for scenic coastal views
  • Bus tours of the island for comprehensive sightseeing
  • Hikes through the lush rainforest
  • Visits to Taniti's active volcano (safely guided)

Transportation

Getting to Taniti.

Arrival by Air

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air. The island is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Taniti is currently in the process of expanding the airport so that larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years.

Arrival by Sea

Some visitors arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week, offering an alternative scenic arrival option.

Ground Transportation

Getting around the island.

Public Buses

Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m. every day. Private buses serve the rest of the island.

Taxis

Taxis are available in Taniti City for convenient point-to-point transportation.

Rental Cars

Rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency located near the airport.

Bikes

Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors throughout the island. Note that helmets are required by law.

Walking

Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay in the area surrounding Merriton Landing, which is also easy to explore on foot.

Frequently Asked Questions

Essential information for your visit.

Electricity

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

Alcohol

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age on Taniti is 18, and the drinking age is not strictly enforced.

Language

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.

Medical Services

There is one hospital and several clinics on the island. The hospital has many multilingual employees to assist international visitors.

Safety

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Visitors should take standard precautions with their belongings.

Holidays

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays. Visitors should plan accordingly.

Currency

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks on the island facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
